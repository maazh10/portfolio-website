This "rigged" nonsense is such a loser mentality. It's truly sad to see such an elite player, such an elite comeback, and such an amazing match get undermined by this sad narrative.

I strongly believe that if a player like Lionel Messi didn't exist in this era, his legacy would be undisputed, and we wouldn't hear this constant outcry fueled mostly by one fanbase. We all know which one.


Cristiano Ronaldo is a great player, and I'm truly happy to see show his excellence on the pitch when he does. But he earns zero respect from me as a sportsman or as a person. To lose in the biggest tournament in the world, take zero responsibility, and show no emotion for your nation, only for yourself, is just sadistic. To constantly create delusional narratives for yourself and your fans or to claim yourself as "the greatest" all over the media is not the attitude of a true great. I believe this mentality propagates to the fans who look up to him.


To be clear, there's nothing wrong with supporting someone and cheering for the player you love watching. But when a player like Cristiano constantly promotes himself through the media and convinces the world that there's a comparison between these two players where there really isn't one and never was, it fuels a generation of people who end up despising a once-in-a-generation, probably once-in-a-lifetime player like Lionel Messi.

I love Steph Curry. He's another athlete I look up to and admire for the way he plays basketball. But there's no delusional Steph Curry fanbase that tries to bring Steph into conversations about the greatest ever alongside LeBron, Jordan, and the others. There's a very simple explanation for that. Steph Curry doesn't go out into the media claiming he's the greatest of all time or why he's better than player X or player Y. He goes out there, gives everything for his team, and has fun doing it. 

That's why I look up to Leo as well. His passion, commitment, and humility are unmatched. At 39 years old, the man is still giving everything he has for his nation after already winning everything there is to win, both with Argentina and individually. He's still breaking record after record, leading the Golden Boot race against players in their prime, and yet whenever the cameras are on him, he doesn't talk about his greatness, his goals, or his achievements. All he says is that he's happy to see his team win.


That's not just a great player. That's a great sportsman. That's a great person.
And we don't even have to get started on his greatness on the pitch. Whether he has a World Cup or not, whether he's on the scoresheet or not, whether he holds the most records or the most trophies, none of it really matters. Once you watch him play, you just know there's no one like him, and there may never be. A player like him may never exist again.


Yet there are people today, wasting their time creating fake controversies instead of appreciating the greatness of what is statistically one of the greatest players the sport has ever seen. My theory has always been that if Messi didn't exist in the same era as Cristiano, none of this would have happened. Messi's status as the undisputed GOAT would have been accepted by everyone. It still should be, but for some people, their egos won't allow it.

Today, watching the incredible performance from him and the team in those final 15 minutes, seeing the grit, the passion, and the determination behind that comeback, only for it to be overshadowed by these ridiculous "rigged" allegations once again, genuinely saddens me. Instead of talking about the brilliance of those goals and that comeback, we're talking about conspiracy theories.

I've always been inspired by Leo because of his story and his passion for the sport. But today, I'm inspired by him even more as I watch him continue doing what he's doing at this age after already winning it all. That's what passion looks like. That's what love for the game and for your country looks like. I try to apply that same mentality to my own career and my own life.

The world seriously needs to start appreciating his greatness because we don't have many years left to watch him play. When he's gone, we'll realize there will never be another player like him. Unfortunately, we can't change the internet or the way people think. People believe whatever fits their agenda. To them, it's always "rigged" whenever reality doesn't.
